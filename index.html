Anatomy & Physiology Launch × Loop Marketing

Where this maps onto Loop Marketing

Loop Marketing formalizes something I did manually, sequentially, and slower for years. Click through each stage to see how the Anatomy & Physiology (A&P) launch actually played out.

A&P initially launched as a print textbook with MindTap, Cengage’s online homework and courseware platform and one of the recurring digital products behind the Cengage Unlimited subscription model. A corresponding lab manual followed a year later, with the textbook and lab manual both available in eBook form. The loop below covers how we took all of it to market.

Express

Partnered with HAPS, the Human Anatomy & Physiology Society, to establish credibility with a skeptical faculty audience. Our source of truth wasn’t product features. It was academic trust.

Positioned A&P around what made it different for faculty evaluating a brand-new entrant: inclusive content and learner support that went beyond the competition.

Amplify

Distributed across HAPS-sponsored author webinars, conference presence, blogs, landing pages, and email campaigns to reach faculty directly, while equipping the sales org through enablement materials built for the same launch.

Internally, coordinated timing across Marketing, Campaigns, Product, Learning & Development, and Sales so the message landed where it needed to at exactly the right time.

Tailor

Built distinct content for two audiences. Faculty got landing pages, a product video, webinars, and a targeted email campaign. Sales got know/show guides, demo guides, and competitive cards.

Targeted messaging specifically for schools already using Cengage’s subscription program or other Cengage Biology materials.

Evolve

Refined positioning continuously, using feedback from faculty in sales calls and focus groups, from reps in the field, and from the HAPS community at conferences, feeding it back into Express as the product matured.

The GTM foundation kept compounding after I moved on, from $500K to $1M+, evidence the loop kept working on its own momentum.

If I were doing this today

I was running this loop manually, sequentially, and slower. This is the framework and the AI tooling I’d reach for to move faster. The loop is also designed to start anywhere depending on what a campaign needs; A&P touched all four continuously, not in strict order.

First 90 days

Listen first, build second

First priority: listen. Deeply understand the product, existing positioning, what’s working and what isn’t, and the people I’d be partnering with across product, sales, partnerships, marketing, and support.

  • Learn the product deeply enough to have genuine opinions about it.
  • Meet key stakeholders across teams and understand how they think about PMM.
  • Audit existing positioning and enablement materials to identify gaps.
  • Have a clear point of view on one or two things I could improve or build, with the right people aligned to start moving on it.
  • Earn the right to those opinions by doing the work first.
  • Have that initiative moving through its first real cycle, with early signals, not final results, to share with the team.
  • Use those signals to refine direction. The same loop mindset I’d bring to any initiative.
  • The specific focus may shift, but the rhythm stays consistent: listen, form a view, test, refine.
